Book Tenth

  • 278—Omitted with the best MSS.
  • 754—Insidiis, iaculo et longe fallente sagitta. The MS. authority is decidedly in favour of this, the more difficult reading; and the hendiadys is not more violent than those in Georg. ii. 192, Aen. iii. 223.

Book Twelfth

  • 218—Tum magis, ut propius cernunt non viribus aequis.

With Ribbeck I believe that there is a gap in the sense here, and have marked one in the translation.

  • 520—Limina with Med. Munera Con.

ll. 612, 613—Omitted with the best MSS.

  • 751—Venator cursu canis et latratibus instat. I take cursu canis as equivalent to currente cane, as in i. 324, spumantis apri cursum clamore prementem.
